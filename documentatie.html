Introduction

MeHe(Mental Health Companion App) is an app designed to manage and mantain a person's mental well-being. The features vary from helping with daily planning to providing informative media and hosting communities. The app encourages the user to plan their day in a productive way by tracking upcoming events, task and setting reminders for them, access different types of relaxation techniques, informative articles and tips and connect with others through communities.

Research Tactics

Questionnaire

We chose this method of research in order to understand the preferences and requirements of individuals concerning mental health apps. Our goal was to get information about the different ways people manage their mental health based on their mental state. The survey looks for the feature people seem to need the most in order to make our app as helpful and user-friendly as possible. That's why, our questionnaire is structured in 3 parts: "Personal Information", "Mental Health Information" and "The App's Features and Functionality".

Competitive Research

Competitive analysis is a critical step in the development and implementation of any app. It consists in evaluating the products and strategies of direct competitors in the market. This process helps developers and businesses understand the landscape in which they are operating, identifying both opportunities and threats. We have researched multiple apps in order to see which features are most common and the public's opinion on them. In the attached PDF we've analysed our competitors based on: variety of coping tools, target audience, price structure, customer catisfaction, strengths and weaknesses.

Identifying Target Audiences

Understanding who your competitors are targeting can provide insights into market segmentation, helping to pinpoint gaps or opportunities. In the developed research, most apps focus mainly on helping adults and young adults (students), but there are also apps with special features for families with kids and couples. MeHe will focus mainly on teens, students and adults, but it's suited for anyone who is looking for some stress relief, clarity and better organizing in their life.

Assessing Strengths and Weaknesses

By analyzing what competitors do well and where they fall short, one can better position their app. Learning from competitors' strengths allows us to incorporate similar features that work while identifying their weaknesses and presents an opportunity to offer something unique or improved that adds value to users and improves their overall experience. The main weaknesses that stood out while analyzing our competitors were complicated and crowded interfaces, hard to use for the average-skilled user. Therefore, a simple, clean interface is to be considered for the MeHe app. Also, the apps' glitches and limited free content were brought to our attention as important problems that need to be avoided and taken into consideration in the app's implementation. Among the most important strengths that we observed in the analyzed apps were: a high variety of tools, flexibility, and customization of the available tools and their offline and free disponibility.

Learning from Competitor Ratings and Feedback

User ratings and reviews of competing apps are gold mines of information. They can reveal what users love and dislike, helping to avoid common pitfalls and bring innovation and improvement on areas that are less satisfactory. For example, if users complain about the complexity of a competing app, offering a simpler, more user-friendly alternative could provide a competitive edge.

Comparing Pricing Structures

All the apps documented in the competitive analysis research require a paid subscription for the user to gain access to the entire variety of coping tools and features. Users often complain about the limited free content available, and also about the high, unaffordable prices for the subscriptions. The MeHe app will offer free access to the entire stack of functionalities, therefore being suited for everyone's pockets.

Sketches

  • First Sketch

    • This sketch represents the "Dashboard" page in our mobile app.

    The "Quick Access" page is the first thing the user sees when going to "Dashboard". It has a menu from where the user can access all the features that are provided to him in order to organise their day and relax through the bookmarks articles/videos or the diary. On the bottom of the page, the user can access the calendar by clicking on the date or they can move ahead or behind by one day.

    The menu lets the user navigate to the desired feature: events, tasks, reminders, goals, trackers, diary and bookmarks. Also, the setting button lets the user personalise their "Quick Access" board by choosing which features will appear.

    The calendar page will appear after the user clicks on the date in the "Quick Access" page. By swiping the user can navigate towards other monts and years. After clicking the "add button", the user will choose between "event", "task" or "reminder" and will be forwarded to the selected page in order to add a new item

    The "Events" and "Tasks" page are similar, showing the user all the events/tasks inputed chronologicaly, except the ones which are flaged as "Important". The "New Event" and "New Task" pages are also similar letting the user personalise the color, if the item is important or if they need a reminder for it.

  • Second Sketch

    • This sketch also represents the "Dashboard" page in our mobile app.

    The "Reminder" and "New Reminder" pages are similar to the "Events" and "Tasks" page, letting the user personalise if they want to receive a notification and how often during the day do they want it.

    "Goals" page displays all the goals the user has inputed, the active ones on top. Every day the user can check the "Check button" if they want to add that day to their progress, updating the progress bar as well.

    The "Trackers" page shows the active trackers the users has, allowing him to create new ones. By clicking on the "Arrow Button" the user will be forwarded to the specific tracker.For example, for the "Habit Tracker" the user will check for every habit if they completed it that day. The user can also access past logs of that tracker from past months.

    The user can choose to create a new dairy in the "Diary" page or to continue writing or inserting pictures in old ones.

    In the "Bookmarks" page the user will have access to the saved videos/articles from the "Explore" page in order to play/read them quicker.

  • Third Sketch

    • This sketch represents the "Login", "Signup", "My Profile", "Community" and "Explore" pages in our mobile app.

    In the "My Profile" page the user is able to modify personal information like username, birthday, profile picture, bio, etc. Also, they can input health details in order to get more personalised results on the "Explore" page and modify their bookmarks.

    The "Community" page offers the possibility of joining new communities based on similar interests. The user can also view the posts in the community they are currently in.

    The "Explore" page is used for finding new articles/videos (example: meditation, relaxing exercises, stretching, breathing exercises and other tips). The page offers the most popular searches to the user when it is accessed.

Conclusion

In summary, the development of the MeHe app consists in creating a comprehensive mental health companion that is accessible and beneficial to a diverse user base. From the initial research stages, including surveys and competitive analysis, to the detailed design and interaction platforms, every step has been aimed at understanding and addressing the unique mental health needs of individuals. The MeHe app stands out in its market due to its inclusive design and user-first approach, which prioritizes simplicity, accessibility, and the comprehensive nature of its features. MeHe is an app designed to support, guide, and enhance the mental well-being of its users every day, and the expected outcome will hopefully be seen through the positive impact MeHe will have on its users, empowering them to lead happier, healthier, and more organized lives.
